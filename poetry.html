Why do I like to write Poetries?

If my memory serves me right, I started writing as early as third grade. Sure, it was all messy and full of grammatical errors, but that's when I fell in love with literary works. I wasn't privileged enough to buy myself a book, but what I learned in school was sufficient for me to start writing on my own. I began writing stories and short poems secretly until I was finally discovered by a teacher in high school. This encouraged me to enter the Division Schools Press Conference under the Feature Writing (English category). Unfortunately, I wasn't capable enough to secure a spot in the top three, and I wasn’t able to compete in the Regional Schools Press Conference.

After some time, my journalism journey stopped because of the COVID-19 pandemic. But that did not hinder me from constantly writing. Once I got my own phone, I started reading on Wattpad and even on Facebook, which, in return, widened my English vocabulary. After the pandemic ended, I was motivated to compete again in the Division Schools Press Conference. With my previous experiences, I was confident enough to compete, but again, unfortunately, I wasn't able to secure a spot. I was quite devastated since it was already my second time competing, and I still didn’t win. Furthermore, my siblings were known as tough contenders and had won numerous competitions, especially in the field of journalism. People's expectations of me felt like a heavy load, and when I didn’t win, I was more disappointed in myself than anybody else.

After a while, a teacher asked me to compete in an English quiz bee. I was very uncertain and lacked confidence and motivation since I had never won any of the competitions I had entered before. But my friend consoled and encouraged me, saying that he and the teacher believed in my capabilities. So, with that thought in mind, I competed in my very first quiz bee. Surprisingly, I won second place, and that made me feel very happy since it was my first win in a while. After that, before I graduated from high school, I competed for the last time in the same category at the Division Schools Press Conference. This time, I didn’t expect much. I simply exerted my best effort and enjoyed writing. And I guess this was my “third time’s a charm” moment because I won first place. I was ecstatic and, most importantly, grateful to God because He allowed me to win before I graduated, and I was able to experience such a wondrous opportunity and competed in the Regionals. My heart thumped uncontrollably due to excitement and fear, but nonetheless, I was very happy with the result.

As I competed in the Regional Schools Press Conference and represented the Ormoc City Division, I made some good friends with whom I shared laughter and disappointments with. Unfortunately, none of us were able to secure a spot for the Nationals. Though we were regretful and devastated, our memories together remained one of my favorites, and I will forever treasure them. The connections I made and the stories we shared together felt like a reward for me. Additionally, while I was in the competition, my heart was surprisingly calm, and I didn’t make any errors. I was quite satisfied with what I wrote since I poured my heart into that piece. Though I did not win, the relationships I built were enough for me to feel satisfied and happy.
